Soil Type

The classification of soil based on texture and composition (e.g., sandy, loamy, clay). It affects water retention, nutrient availability, and root penetration.

pH Level

A measure of soil acidity or alkalinity on a scale from 0 to 14. Most crops thrive in slightly acidic to neutral soil (pH 6.0â€“7.5).

Organic Matter (%)

The percentage of decomposed plant and animal materials in the soil. It improves soil fertility, structure, and moisture retention.

Nitrogen Content (kg/ha)

Amount of nitrogen in kilograms per hectare. Nitrogen is essential for plant growth and leaf development, especially in early stages.

Phosphorus Content (kg/ha)

Phosphorus level in kilograms per hectare. Crucial for root growth, flowering, and fruiting in plants.

Potassium Content (kg/ha)

The potassium available in the soil, measured in kg/ha. It helps with water regulation, disease resistance, and overall plant vigor.
